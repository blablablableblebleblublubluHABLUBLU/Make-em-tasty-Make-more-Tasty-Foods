Shoyu Ramen:


About Shoyu Ramen:

Shoyu Ramen (醤油ラーメン), also known as Tokyo ramen, It consists of a soy sauce flavor and a clear soup broth served with medium-thin curly noodles.The light but delicious soup stock is made from chicken bones/pork bones and occasionally kombu seaweed, dried anchovies, katsuobushi or knowkn as Bonito Flakes. This will not be a Shoyu Ramen without these simple toppings of chashu (a Japanese pork belly), pickled bamboo shoots, green onions, and some Nori seaweeds.

Shoyu Ramen is considered an old school classic in japan. In 1910 Shoyu Ramen was first introduced in Rairaiken in Asakusa, Tokyo. At that time, Shoyu Ramen was called the "Tokyo Ramen".

Out of all the dishes sold at Rairaiken, Shoyu Ramen was its best seller. Shoyu Ramen became the catalyst for the Ramen's popularity throughout Japan.


Ingredients:

>PORK LEG BONES-Among the pork bones, thigh bones and knuckles is the best to make a sweet, flavorful pork bone for ramen.

>PORK BELLY STRIPS (WITHOUT SKIND/RIND)-To make the chashu, we will be needing 2 long narrow pork belly strips measuring 1½ inches x 2 inches x 10 inches or 3.8 cm x 5cm x 25 cm.

>RENDERED CHICKEN FAT-The soup broth will taste even better after adding some chicken fat, so you better not forget this ingredient.

>AROMATICS: ONION, GARLIC, GINGER, AND TOKYO NEGI (LONG GREEN ONION)-This will help your ramen more flavorful and have a good aroma on it. if you cant find tokyo negi, you can use green onions/scallions as a susbtitution.

>SHOYU TARE (SAUCE)-You can make shoyu tare by mixing soy sauce, sake, mirin, and salt.

FRESH RAMEN NOODLES-Of course we will be needing some fresh ramen noodles. this wouldn't be a ramen without its signature noodles!

>RAMEN TOPPINGS-For toppings, we will be needing some green onions, menma (seasoned bamboo shoots) and Nori seaweeds.

Procedure:

1. Start by making the soup broth by boiling the pork leg bones, aromatics, and pork belly in the water for 2 hours.

2. Right before the 2 hour mark, take out some soup broth and combine it with soy sauce, sake, mirin, and salt in another pot to make a chashu sauce.

3. at the 2 hour mark, add the chicken fat and the green parts of the tokyo negi (long green onions) to a pot of the soup broth and cook for another hour.

4. After the 3 hours of boiling the soup broth, take out the pork belly, and continue boiling the other parts for the next 3 hours.

5. Add the cooked pork belly to the chashu sauce and braise for 2 hours.

6. After cooking for 2 hours, remove the chashu from the sauce and let it cool completely before putting it to the refrigerator. Save this chashu sauce for the ramen'shoyu tare.

7. After the 6 hours of boiling the soup broth, remove the remaining bones and aromatics, and the soup broth is now ready!

8. To serve this, slice the chilled chashu into thin slices and cook the ramen noodles. After that, add the shoyu tare, piping hot soup broth and ramen noodles to the bowl. Add the toppings (green onions, menma, and nori seaweed. you can also some boiled eggs if you want.) And now you're done! Your own Shoyu Ramen is now finished!

Bibimbap:

image:www.thespruceeats.com

About Bibimbap:

Bibimbap (비빔밥) comes from a combined words of "Bibim" which means mixing ingredients together and "bap" means rice, so the actual translation of Bibimbap is "Mixed Rice". Bibimbap consists of various ingredients such as rice, vegetables, and usually a protein such as beef or tofu. Topped with a fried egg and gochujang (Korean chili paste). Bibimbap is a healthy, balanced, nutritious, and colorful meal.

Bibimbap was originally known as "goldongbwan" (It means mixing together various objects with already cooked rice) In the 1400s and then later called "bubimbap" in the late 1800s. between the 1800s and 1900s bibimbap significally changed in terms of ingredients added to the bowl.

Ordinary people ate bibimbap on the eve of the lunar new year as the people that time felt that they needed to get rid of the leftovers. They came up with a solution to their problem which is to put all of the leftovers in a bowl of rice and mix them all together. The farmers ate the Bibimbap during the farming seasin and it was the easiest food to make for a large amount of people, bibimbap was also served to a king.


Ingredients:

>SOME VEGGIES!- We need some veggies for our bibimbap. We need seasoned spinach, seasoned bean sprouts, shiitake mushrooms, and some carrots. You can also add some other veggies too to make it more nutritious and delicious.

>SALT AND PEPPER- You need these 2 ingredients to make your bibimbap more flavorful.

>STEAMED RICE- Of course we needed rice for this recipe!

>BIBIMBAP SAUCE- For the sauce you will be needing a 2 tbsp of Gochujang (korean chili paste), 1tbsp of sesame oil, 1 tbs of sugar, 1tbsp of water, 1 tbsp of roasted sesame seeds, 1tbsp of Vinegar, 1tsp of minced garlic. Mix all of them.

>TOPPINGS- You can add some sunny side up egg on top, Nori sheets, and some sesame seeds.

>MEAT SAUCE- You need 1tbsp of soy sauce, 1tbsp of sesame oil, 1 tbsp of brown sugar, ¼ tsp of minced garlic.

>BEEF-cut the beef into thin slices/strips and marinate it in the meat sauce for 30 minutes.


Procedure:

1. For the beef, marinate it with the meat sauce for about 30 minutes. Add some cooking oil into a pan and cook the meat on a medium high to high heat, cook it for 3 to 5 minutes.

2. As for the veggies, cleanse/rinse them and peel the carrots. slice the carrots and mushroom into thin pieces and add some cooking oil in the pan and ¼ sea salt and cook the carrots, mushroom, spinach, and the bean sprout for about 2 to 3 minutes.

3. As for the serving, put the rice into the bowl and add the meat, asorted veggies, seasoned seweed, bibimbap sauce, and if you want you can also put some sunny side up, nori sheets, and also some kimchi for more flavor. And now you're ready to eat your own Bibimbap!


Chinese Dumpling (Xiao Long Bao):

image:legendaryspicemn.com


About Xiao Long Bao:

Xiao Long Bao (小籠包) or "little basket bun" is a type of a chinese tangbao (汤包) that is traditionally made in a xiaolong, a small bamboo steaming basket. Xiao long bao has a delicate skin with the savory meat filling and a high umami soup holding within the pleated pouch. Xiao long bao originates in the city of Changzhou in Jiangsu province and is also an iconic dish in jiangnan cuisine.

Xiao Long Bao is created by Wan Hua Tea House during the years of 1820 to 1850 (The years of Daoguang Emperor). Xiao Long Bao evolved from the guantangbao of kaifeng, In henan province which is the capital city of Northern Song Dynasty. There are numerous styles of xiao long bao in the jiangnan cuisine. The shanghai style of Xiao long Bao was orginated in nanxiang.

Xiaong Long Bao became popular and loved by the people around the world.


Ingredients:

>ASPIC- The first thing you need to make is the aspic, this will create the soup in the xiao long bao. To make this, you will be needing ½ lb of pork skin (225g),cut them into a 1-inch strips, 1 lb (450g) of pork neck bones (You want neck bone that still have meat on them), water, 1 scallion (cut them into 3 pieces), 1 tbsp of Shaoxing wine or dry cooking sherry. (Don't worry about using powdered gelatin, the gelatin will come right from the bones and skin.)

>DOUGH- 1 cup of all purpose flour (130g) and 6 tbsp of warm water(90 ml).

>THE FILLING- For the filling, we will be needing these ingredients: 1 pound (450g) of ground pork, 2 tbsp of shaoxing wine or dry cooking sherry, ¾ tsp of salt, ½ tsp of sesame oil, ¾ tsp of sugar, 3 tsp of soy sauce, 3 tbsp of water, A pinch of ground white pepper, 1 tbsp of minced ginger, 1 heaping cup of your aspic, diced into ½-inch pieces.


Procedure:

1.For the aspic, add the pork skin and pork bones in a small pot and cover with cold water. bring to a rolling boil. immediately drain and rinse off the bones and the skin. rinse out the pot and put everything back, Add 4 cups (950 ml) of a water along with the ginger, scallion and wine. bring the pot to a boil and put the heat to low. cover the pot and let it boil for about 2 hours. after 2 hours, turn off the heat and let the soup to cool, once its now completely cold strain the liquid into a bowl and cover the liquid and refrigerate it overnight.

2. lets now head to the dough! in a mixing bowl, add the flour and warm water. Wrok and knead the dough for 15-20 minutes and it should be very smooth and soft. After that, cover the dough with a clean cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

3. As for the filling, you need to take your ground pork and put it into a food processor and pulse it for 30-60 seconds until the pork looks like a paste. In a mixing bowl, add the pork and all the rest of the ingredients. Mix all together thoroughly for 2 minutes and the mixture should look like a light and airy paste. Gently fold in the diced aspic and do not over mix it so the aspic won't become all crushed. Cover it and transfer the filling to the refrigerator for 15 minutes.

4. Lightly dust a clean work surface with flour and roll the dough into a long cylinder shape about 1 inch. Cut the dough into a small equal pieces weighing about 11 grams each.

5. get the filling from the fridge and put the filling in the dough and make a little "bag", make sure the top is completely sealed.

6. Prepare your bamboo steamer, of you don't have one you can use a regular steamer (if you're using the bamboo steamer you must brush the discs with oil first). Place your dumplings in the steamer and make sure they're apart from eachother and steam over high heat for 8 minutes.

7. After steaming, immediately serve it and now your Xiao Long Bao is ready! You can also use a Chinese black vinger as a Dip. If you don't have one, Soy sauce will do! Tip:Eat it slowly if you don't want the broth to splash on your face!


Kare-Kare:

image:www.pinterest.com


About Kare-Kare:

Kare-kare is a Philippine curry (the word "Kare" derives from "curry"). Kare-kare features a thick savory peanut sauce. it is generally made from a base of stewed oxtail, beef tripe, pork hocks, calve's feet, pig's feet or trotters, various cuts of pork, beef stew meat, and ocassionally offal. Kare-kare also has veggies on it such as eggplant, chinese cabbage, green beans, asparagus beans (sitaw) are added to the Kare-kare (some people in the philippines add banana flower in their Kare-kare). The Kare-kare's signature yellow sauce was made from a ground roasted peanuts or peanut butter, onions, and garlic. Of course, Kare-kare wouldn't be more delicious without its "bagoong' (shrimp paste). It is said that kare-kare is orginated in Pampanga (the culinary Capital of the Philippines) though, There's alot of theory about this dish like it orginated from the moro which is the indigenous people in the Philippines, some said the sauce came from trade ships in Mexico, and some also said it came from Indian settlers during the British occupation of manila. But who knows? maybe one of them are actually true!


Ingredients:

>MEATS- Prepare 3 lbs oxtail and cut in 2 inches slices, you can also use tripe, pata, or beef slice. You can use any meat you want.

>VEGGIES- As for the veggies, you will be needing 1 piece small banana flower bud (slice it), a bundle of string beans (cut into 2 inches slices), Eggplants( slice into thin pieces).

>SAUCE- Prepare a 1 cup of ground peanut, ½ cup of peanut butter, 34 Ounces of water, ½ cup of annatto seeds (soaked in a cup of water), 1 tbsp minced garlic, 1 chopped onion, ½ cup of toasted ground rice.

>SALT AND PEPPER- To add more flavor to the kare-kare.

>SHRIMP PASTE- Kare-Kare's best combo is a shrimp paste especially when its a spicy one!


Procedure:

1. In a large pot, bring the water to boil and when it is now boiling, put the meat followed by the onions in the pot and simmer it for 2-3 hours.

2. Once the meat is tender, Add the ground peanut, peanut butter, and coloring (the water from annato mixture) and let it simmer 5-7 minutes. After that, add the toasted ground rice and simmer 5 minutes.

3. On a separate pan, Saute the garlic then add the banana flower, eggplant, and string beans, cook for about 5 minutes.

4. As for the serving, transfer the all of the ingredients in one pot and prepare some shrimp paste to the side and now you're ready to eat!!
